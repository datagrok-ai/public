Valerij Talagayev is a Scientific Application Developer at Datagrok, focused on cheminformatics and bioinformatics software. He holds a PhD in Molecular Drug Design from Freie Universität Berlin (Wolber Lab), where he leads the development of OpenMMDL — an open-source interface to OpenMM for molecular dynamics simulations of protein-ligand complexes.

His research spans structure- and ligand-based CADD: he has identified novel nM-range TLR8 inhibitors and discovered candidates against immune checkpoints including ICOS, LAG-3, and TIM-3 through pharmacophore-based virtual screening. Prior to Datagrok, he worked at AI|ffinity (Prague) building deepHitExplorer, a computational hit-discovery platform combining NMR, AI, and cheminformatics, and contributed to Hamiltonian Replica Exchange MD simulations with NMR restraints.

Valerij is an active open-source contributor: he participated in Google Summer of Code 2024 (MDAnalysis, 2D small-molecule visualization) and mentored GSoC 2025 students on HBond interactions and ProLIF visualization. He holds a Pharmacy degree from Philipps-Universität Marburg, where he began his journey into computational chemistry studying GPCR–antipsychotic interactions.
